The Wild Island of Koh Phangan

Sunday morning started early. Woke up at 6 AM and felt more than ready to finally leave Bangkok.

A short flight to Surat Thani, a bus to Don Sak Sakon Port and we boarded the ferry, which took us directly to the island of Koh Phangan. Beautiful views on the way – crystal blue water, nice beaches surrounded with palm trees and green hills.

Once we arrived to the port town called Thong Sala, I could really feel the humidity on my body. However, it felt way better than the burning Bangkok’s sun. Everything in this island just feels so relaxing. Everybody’s chilling and riding their motorbikes around. Even the dogs seem super chill catching sun on the beach. Locals don’t try to sell everything to you on the streets. And they even have vegetarian dishes in most of the places. No more shrimps and crabs for every meal. Tofu all the way!
Having been here for 3 days, Koh Phangan feels like it’s just the place to be – palms, beaches, sunsets, interesting people and even some proper civilized facilities, so you don’t feel completely lost of where you are in the world.

It’s simply sun, sea, incredible nature, smiles, good vibes and amazing things happening out of nowhere.


We started our first morning early with a refreshing swim in the South China Sea. Immediately after that, while sipping first cup of coffee, an incredibly friendly Jesus-looking guy appeared and told us many great stories and the best places to visit in this island. Be blessed, my friend!

So we packed our things, put on some hip Adidas slippers and took a wild 10 kilometer walk by half beach, half jungle road until the crystal blue Zen Beach in the Northwest of Koh Phangan.

Afterwards, we made it back to Thong Sala and got a nice place right on the beach. It even has a pool in the middle of all the Bungalows. All this for 65 euros a week per person. Can’t complain about the price/quality ratio.

Most of the places in this island are super cheap as well. Meals seem to be even cheaper than Bangkok and I haven’t ever paid more than 3 euros for a delicious Thai dinner with beer.

The island is also famous for it’s parties. Starting from the well-known Full Moon Party at the beach of Haad Riin, gathering 20.000 people in one place to alternative techno, house or trance parties at the secret beaches and jungles. A review of that and way more is definitely coming in my next posts!

I love it so far and it seems like there’s plenty more just waiting around the corner.
