Bangkok

As some of you know, I am travelling together with my homie bigboi Marat and the very first destination in our Asian journey was the capital of Thailand – Bangkok, where we stayed for 6 nights.
The city is famous for many things – temples, food, parties, smiles… You name it.

Before we arrived here, we had a stopover in Kiev, Ukraine, where we had a nice walk around the coooold city and a few beers, which turned into even more beers. Thanks to my amazing Russian skills… When I wanted to pay, I accidentally ordered 4 more beers for us… За здоровя!

Stepping out of the plane after 2 flights and a total of 18 hours travelling time was a relief.

I expected the heat to strike stronger but it actually felt alright for my dark Lithuanian body. +30C is not that bad in here compared to home. Anyways, the heat and the sun is never bad.

Alright, back to Bangkok – half an hour’s UBER ride and we were in our hotel where we immediately headed for the pool. Feels great to swim outside in January!


Afterwards we paid a visit to the famous Khao San Road – backpackers’ paradise filled with bars, clubs and street vendors selling you anything from Pad Thai meals to fried scorpions on a stick and Ping Pong show tickets.

Being a vegetarian in here seems to be a hard mission. Good that I still eat fish and seafood, otherwise I wouldn’t really find many meals suitable for me. Pad Thai with shrimps is great for sure! And it’s usually around 1 or 2 euros, depending on where you buy it.

We explored many other things around here – markets, temples, parks and many different parts of this huge city. Here are some pics:

You can find anything you imagine here in Bangkok as it is home to more than 9 million people and they definitely have every kind of entertainment you like. We even found some nice house and techno clubs, which ended with massive hangover the next day. Pay a visit to Residence de Canal or Safe Room if you’re here but don’t even try to do drugs – police is strict and you’re gonna go to jail if you’re caught, which must be a terrible experience as they don’t even give you meals there if you don’t have the money. Just drink and enjoy. Alcohol is not that expensive in here. Around 2.5 euros for a beer in a bar is the usual. And it’s half this price in 7 Eleven.

I don’t want this post to end up being too long as everybody knows how bad it sucks to read the super long blogs. You better read a book, you know. So I will simply sum it up in a few words, let you enjoy the pictures and you can always feel free to ask me for any advice later on.
Bangkok is a huuuge city full of anything you want. I loved the calm feeling in their wonderful Buddhist temples, the food, the smiles, the sun, the beautiful skyline and buildings but I hated the traffic, the trash and the feeling that it’s just too much of everything. Sometimes it takes like 5 minutes to cross a street as nobody really cares about the crosswalks or even traffic lights on the roads. They also try to sell you everything – especially the Tuk Tuk rides. We even had a police officer come to us, tell the notorious story that The Grand Palace is closed for today, which of course was not (you can always check the opening times online or at the entrance) and offering us his friend’s service to drive us around other attractions for a fee. This kind of things and corruption is a big thing in here.

I can also say that one week in the capital is a bit too long and you should be fine with staying for 2-3 days in total. Maybe it’s just me because I simply dream of reaching the beach ASAP.

Finally, the city is really cheap and 10-15 euros can easily take you through the day. It is including food, drinks, transportation and some entertainment. Of course, being a tourist you will spend more for all the fun activities, sightseeing and parties. However, all of this is at least 2-3 times cheaper than in Europe.

We’re off to chill in Koh Phangan island tomorrow.
